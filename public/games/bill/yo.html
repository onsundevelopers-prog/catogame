$561.2 BILLION

John D. Rockefeller

America’s first billionaire owned incredible amounts of oil reserves and at least 2% of the entire American economy. He owned over 90% of American pipelines by the early 1880s.

The Shopping List: You could buy every single team in the NFL, NBA, and MLB, and still have over $400 Billion left for snacks.
The Stack: If you stacked his wealth in $100 bills, it would reach 380 miles high—well past the International Space Station.
$2 TRILLION

Catherine the Great

Seizing the Russian throne in 1760, she held investments up to 5% of the Russian GDP. She played a crucial role in military strategy and fighting inflation.

The Shopping List: You could buy the entire company of Apple, hand it to a friend, and then buy Netflix for yourself.
The Stack: This amount of gold would weigh roughly 40,000 tons—enough to build a solid gold version of the Statue of Liberty.
$5.8 TRILLION

Augustus Caesar

One of Rome's greatest emperors, his empire controlled 30% of the world’s GDP. Personally, one-fifth of the entire Empire's wealth went directly into his pocket.

The Shopping List: You could pay for every single person in the United States to go to a private college for 4 years, twice.
The Stack: If you laid his $5.8 Trillion in $1 bills end-to-end, they would reach from the Earth to the Sun.
$25 TRILLION

Empress Wu Zetian

The only female regent in China's history. The Tang Empire produced 25% of the world's wealth, translated to roughly $25 trillion in today's currency.

The Shopping List: You could buy every single home in the United Kingdom. Not one house—every single house in the entire country.
The Stack: The physical volume of this much cash would be larger than the Great Pyramid of Giza.
$120 TRILLION

Genghis Khan

Ruler of the largest land empire ever. With trillions in diamonds and gold, his assets were worth more than the entire modern world's economy combined.

The Shopping List: You literally own the world. You could buy every building, every car, and every business on the planet and still have change.
The Stack: A cube of gold this valuable would be the size of a 30-story skyscraper.

The Unknowns

We don't even know how much some leaders had. Estimates for some ancient rulers reach into the quadrillions. At that point, the money is just a number.
